No Native Mac App

KDE Connect is a Linux app ported to other platforms. On macOS, it uses a non-native framework resulting in poor battery usage, sluggish UI, and constant crashes.

📞

No WhatsApp Call Support

KDE Connect completely misses WhatsApp calls. You can't accept, reject, or manage WhatsApp calls from your Mac — a critical gap for most users.

💬

No SMS Composer

KDE Connect grays out the "New Message" button — you cannot start a new SMS thread from Mac. You can only read existing ones.

🗂️

No Android File Browser

KDE Connect cannot browse your phone's filesystem from Mac. You can't navigate folders, preview files, or download specific files wirelessly.

🔄

No Resume Support

KDE Connect cannot resume interrupted file transfers. If your WiFi drops for even a second, the entire transfer fails and you have to start over from 0%. Bounce Connect handles brief interruptions with seamless auto-resume.

🧹

No Auto-Cleanup

KDE Connect often leaves orphaned ".part" files consuming space on your Mac after a failed transfer. It lacks a cleanup mechanism to wipe temporary files after a connection drop.

🔔

Passive Notifications Only

KDE Connect just mirrors notifications without letting you interact with them. Bounce Connect brings a full cross-device notification bridge with inline replies, supported Android actions from Mac, and two-way dismiss sync that self-heals after reconnect.

📋

Manual Clipboard Triggers

KDE Connect requires you to manually activate clipboard sync from an Android notification tile. Bounce Connect uses Android's native Share system — no manual triggers needed.

🔋

Massive Background Battery Drain

KDE Connect constantly scans for networks in the background. It lacks the ability to lock connections to specific WiFi zones, leading to unnecessary battery consumption when you are away from your Mac.

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about using Bounce Connect as a KDE Connect alternative on Mac.

Is there a KDE Connect alternative for Mac? +

Yes — Bounce Connect is purpose-built as the best KDE Connect alternative for macOS. Written 100% in native Swift, it supports SMS syncing, WhatsApp call management, notification inline replies, universal clipboard, wireless file transfers, and a remote Android file browser — all features missing or limited in KDE Connect on Mac.

What does KDE Connect not support on Mac? +

KDE Connect has no native macOS app — it runs on a ported framework, causing poor performance and battery drain. It also lacks: WhatsApp call support, the ability to compose new SMS messages, a remote Android file browser, inline notification replies, and clipboard image sync. Bounce Connect covers all of these.

How do I connect my Android phone to my Mac with Bounce Connect? +

Download Bounce Connect on your Mac (macOS 11+) and Bounce Companion on your Android phone (Android 8.0+). Open both apps, scan the QR code shown on Mac using the Android app, and your devices pair instantly over local WiFi. Both must be on the same WiFi network.

Can I send Android SMS from my Mac using Bounce Connect? +

Yes — Bounce Connect provides a full iMessage-style SMS interface on Mac, including the ability to start new threads and choose which SIM card to send from (dual-SIM support). Note: MMS photo attachments are not fully supported yet.

Does Bounce Connect support WhatsApp calls on Mac? +

Yes. You can accept, reject, or mute both regular cellular calls and WhatsApp calls from your Mac. Note: audio is routed through your Android phone's current audio device (e.g. Bluetooth earbuds) — audio routing to Mac is not available.

Does Bounce Connect send my data to the cloud? +

No. Bounce Connect is 100% local. All messages, notifications, clipboard data, and files are transferred directly between your Mac and Android over your local WiFi network using AES-256-GCM encryption. Nothing passes through external servers or cloud storage.

Why do my devices keep disconnecting? +

The most common cause is Android battery optimization killing the Bounce Companion app in the background. Fix it: Settings → Battery → Apps → Bounce Companion → Unrestricted. Also enable Allow background activity. Both settings are required for a stable connection.
